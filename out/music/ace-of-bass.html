Album Case Study

Ace of Bass

Ace of Bass is the kind of title only Jeff Berlin could probably get away with straight-faced. It lands somewhere between confidence, pun, and challenge.

Jeff Berlin2005Studio AlbumJeff: Leader, bass

Why It Matters

This album is worth calling out because it opens a little discographic rabbit hole. MusicBrainz treats Ace of Bass and Aneurythms as the same release group, which makes the project interesting beyond the music itself.

That matters for this archive because it shows Jeff's solo catalog has alternate-release history, not just a neat row of isolated records.

Musically, the track names suggest a playful, self-aware Jeff Berlin record, which fits the broader pattern of him mixing serious musicianship with titles that keep things human.

Quick Snapshot

  • Discogs lists Ace of Bass as a 2005 Jeff Berlin album issued in Japan.
  • MusicBrainz identifies the release group as the same body of work later issued as Aneurythms.
  • The album carries 10 tracks including 'Porky & Beans', 'Saab Story', and 'Bass Boys'.

A discography crossover point

Ace of Bass and Aneurythms are linked at the release-group level in MusicBrainz.

Ten-track solo set

Discogs lists a full 10-song program rather than a side project or EP.

Pure Jeff title energy

The title lands as equal parts swagger and joke, which is exactly why it works.

Listen For

Porky & Beans

A title that tells you immediately this record will not be humorless.

Saab Story

Another reminder that Jeff's writing titles often have a specific private-world feel.

Bass Boys

The closer reads almost like Jeff commenting on the scene from inside it.
